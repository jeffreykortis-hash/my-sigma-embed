The Four Sigma Pillars ‚Äî Your Brainstorm Framework

Every enhancement must map to a pillar. No orphan features.

Pillar 01 ¬∑ Spreadsheet

Familiarity & Flexibility

Ask yourself: What does a manager do after they read this table today?
Pillar 02 ¬∑ Cloud Scale

Warehouse-Native Power

The charts are snapshots. Propose at least one live, warehouse-computed metric ‚Äî weighted pipeline, days-in-stage risk, rolling 90-day attainment. The point: this runs on 10M rows the same as 10K, and it's always current.

Pillar 03 ¬∑ AI & Intelligence

From Descriptive to Intelligent

Pillar 04 ¬∑ Workflow & Action

This is the differentiator. Propose one trigger: a high-risk deal fires a Slack alert to the manager; a discount approval happens inside Sigma and writes back to the warehouse; a weekly summary emails itself to the board in the format they already use.

Workflow Deep Dive ‚Äî What You Need to Know

Workflow is the pillar most teams underestimate. Don't.

Sigma's workflow capability means the dashboard is no longer a destination ‚Äî it's a trigger. Here's how to think about it and what to communicate to the customer:

1

Condition is Met

An AI flag fires, a threshold is crossed, a manager edits a cell, or a scheduled time arrives.

2

Sigma Detects It

Sigma monitors the underlying data in the warehouse ‚Äî no polling, no middleware required.

3

Action Fires

Slack alert, email summary, write-back to the warehouse, PDF delivery ‚Äî the system moves, not the human.

4

Person Acts

The rep, manager, or exec receives exactly what they need, in context, at the right moment.

5

Loop Closes

That action can trigger another write-back or update ‚Äî creating a living, self-maintaining process.
